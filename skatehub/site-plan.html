Overview

Purpose

The purpose of this website is to inform skaters how to build a skateboard, when and where skating events will be held, as well as who the top skaters currently are around the world. It is meant to be a skaters online go-to destination providing them with valuable resources, inspiration, and a supportive community to enhance their skatebaording journey.

Audience

This website is meant for teens/young adults getting into skateboarding. This is to help provide more informatin about the sport and what it entails. This is an imformative website for beginner and intermediate level skaters. This audience will most likely access this website on their phone or ipad.

[The Lab]

HOW TO SET IT UP A Beginners Guide to Master The Art Of Skateboard Building Deck: This is your board. Decks are typically made out of plywood and sometimes composites or fiberglass. The more layers of wood it has the stronger of a deck it will be. The average sizes are 7.25, 7.5, 7.75, 8, 8.25, 8.38, 8.5, 8.6, and 9. Different brands also have different styles of concaves as well as different tails. The more concave a board has the harder it will flick. Some decks have twin tails meaning both sides of the deck are the same. Some decks have odd shapes to them which are typically cruiser style or decks you ride in bowls/vert style skating. Grip Tape: Grip tape is the material you put on top of your board. This material is typically made out of sand paper. Depending on the brand, some grip will be a lot more coarse than other brands. Once you've skated a couple different brands you will get a feel for what coarse grip you like skating. Trucks: This will contain information on trucks. Wheels: This will contain information on wheels. Bearings: This will contain information on bearings. Hardware: This will contain information on hardware.
